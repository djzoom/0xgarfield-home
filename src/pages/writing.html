Notes from the audio category I am building.

Irregular long-form essays on sound as an interface to the nervous system, what AI changes about audio, and what twenty years of shipping audio products taught about the next category. New essays land here, then go out by email.

  • Drafting · 2026

    Audio as dose, not delivery.

    A thesis essay. Most of what we call "audio" today moves signal from a source to an ear. The next category treats sound as a dose: engineered to produce a specific state in a specific person. Why this is finally tractable in 2026, and what it changes about products, hardware, and the listening contract.

  • Drafting · 2026

    What we learned shipping PILO to tens of thousands of sleepers.

    Nine years of shipping a music-based sleep product through Brookstone, JD, and Amazon US/Japan — what the data actually said about how music works at the edge of sleep, and what it implied about the AI layer that comes next.

  • Drafting · 2026

    State-adaptive audio: what AI changes about sound design.

    Sound design has historically been static — written once for a generic listener. AI makes it possible to read the listener and shape audio in response, in real time. A working sketch of the architecture, the open problems, and the early Talktalk results.
