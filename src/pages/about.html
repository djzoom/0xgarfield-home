About

Garfield Wang — Founder, Soundario.

I build AI-native audio systems for how people speak, focus, and perform.

I'm Garfield Wang, founder of Soundario. My work sits between human behavior and product execution: the parts of experience that people immediately feel but teams often struggle to specify. I have spent twenty years approaching that problem from different angles — first as a broadcaster, then as a music producer, then as an audio hardware founder. The current chapter is AI.

Soundario's first product, PILO, is a music-based sleep system. It has been recognized by Red Dot, A' Design, Golden Pin, Plus X, and K-Design, and sold through Brookstone, JD, and Amazon US and Japan. Run Baby Run®, a cadence-music IP I developed for runners, was adopted by Nike as the official soundtrack for the Shanghai Marathon and entered the runner training package; that work led to me serving as Music Production Director for the Nike Shanghai Marathon.

Before all of that, I spent over a decade inside national-scale audio institutions. I led music programming and original production at China Radio International Hit FM. I anchored and supervised music at Phoenix U Radio in Hong Kong. I contributed music writing to The New York Times Chinese Edition. Along the way I conducted long-form interviews with artists including Mark Ronson, Maroon 5, Avril Lavigne, Suede, Eels, and Cui Jian. I have also served on a sound jury at the Emmy Awards.

Soundario today is building the next layer: software that senses a listener's state — physiological, attentional, behavioral — and shapes audio in response, in real time. The thesis hasn't changed in twenty years. Sound is the most under-engineered interface to the human nervous system, and almost everything called "audio" today is delivery: pipes moving signal to ear. I'm interested in audio as dose: sound engineered to produce a specific state in a specific person.

The category, in three axes

  • ExpressionHow people speak — Talktalk and related real-time tools for spoken expression under pressure.
  • AttentionHow people focus — adaptive audio that reads attentional state and shapes sound to support focus and learning.
  • PerformanceHow people sleep, recover, and physically perform — the Soundario thesis from PILO and Run Baby Run®, extended through AI.
